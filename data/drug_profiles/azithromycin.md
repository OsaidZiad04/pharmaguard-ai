# Azithromycin

This local knowledge-base profile is neutral educational content for pharmacist-support workflows. It is not patient-specific advice and must not be used as a final medication reference.

## Overview

Azithromycin is a medication commonly discussed in pharmacy settings as an antibacterial medicine. The pharmacist must verify the exact product, strength, route, directions, duration, allergy history, patient context, and local reference guidance before counseling.

This profile supports local retrieval tests and draft counseling workflows. It does not define patient-specific appropriateness, indication, or final use instructions.

## Common Uses

- Infection-related counseling may be discussed only after the pharmacist confirms the indication, prescription details, and patient-specific factors.
- The pharmacist should verify formulation, route, duration, allergy history, current medicines, and whether the prescription directions are complete.
- The pharmacist should review relevant heart-rhythm history or interacting medicines when appropriate.

## General Counseling Points

- Ask the patient to follow the final pharmacist-approved label directions.
- Encourage the patient to ask what to do if a dose is missed, based on pharmacist-approved guidance and local policy.
- Remind the patient to contact the pharmacist if directions, duration, or formulation handling are unclear.
- Explain that this draft must be checked against the confirmed prescription and approved references before patient-facing use.

## Safety Notes

- Pharmacist review should include allergy history, current medications, pregnancy status when relevant, heart-rhythm context when relevant, and liver-related context when relevant.
- Low confidence prescription text, unclear duration, or missing allergy information should trigger pharmacist review before counseling.
- This profile does not confirm indication, patient suitability, or final treatment decisions.

## When to Refer to Pharmacist or Physician

- Refer for pharmacist assessment when the patient reports allergy concerns, heart-rhythm history, interacting medicines, unclear directions, or difficulty using the formulation.
- Refer to an appropriate clinician according to local policy when symptoms are severe, worsening, persistent, or outside the pharmacist's scope.
- Escalate when allergy history, current medicines, pregnancy status, age, heart-rhythm context, or prescription duration is missing.

## Patient Questions to Ask

- Have you ever had an allergy or reaction to azithromycin or related medicines?
- What other medicines, supplements, or non-prescription products are you taking?
- Do you have heart-rhythm history or other factors the pharmacist should review?
- Are the directions, duration, and formulation instructions clear after pharmacist review?

## Knowledge Base Limitations

- This local profile is a limited educational reference for retrieval testing.
- It does not assess infection type, patient suitability, or therapy selection.
- It does not provide patient-specific dosing, clinical assessment, or final treatment decisions.
