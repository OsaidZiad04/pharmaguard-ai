# Diclofenac

This local knowledge-base profile is neutral educational content for pharmacist-support workflows. It is not patient-specific advice and must not be used as a final medication reference.

## Overview

Diclofenac is a medication commonly discussed in pharmacy settings for pain or inflammation support workflows. The pharmacist must verify the exact product, route, formulation, strength, directions, patient context, and local reference guidance before counseling.

This profile supports local retrieval tests and draft counseling workflows. It does not define patient-specific appropriateness or final use instructions.

## Common Uses

- Pain or inflammation support counseling may be discussed after the pharmacist confirms the product and patient-specific risk factors.
- The pharmacist should distinguish topical and oral formulations and review other pain, inflammation, or anti-inflammatory products.
- Condition-only terms should not be treated as a reason to select diclofenac without pharmacist review.

## General Counseling Points

- Ask the patient to follow the final pharmacist-approved label directions.
- Encourage the patient to ask about formulation-specific instructions for the confirmed product.
- Remind the patient to ask before combining diclofenac with other pain, fever, cold, flu, or anti-inflammatory products.
- Explain that this draft must be checked against the confirmed product and approved references before patient-facing use.

## Safety Notes

- Pharmacist review should include allergy history, pregnancy status, age, stomach or bleeding risk factors, kidney-related context, heart-related context, and current medications.
- Low confidence product text, unclear route, unclear duration, or missing patient context should trigger pharmacist review before counseling.
- This profile does not confirm patient suitability or final treatment decisions.

## When to Refer to Pharmacist or Physician

- Refer for pharmacist assessment when the patient reports allergy concerns, pregnancy, stomach bleeding concerns, kidney-related history, heart-related history, or interacting medicines.
- Refer to an appropriate clinician according to local policy when symptoms are severe, persistent, unusual, or outside the pharmacist's scope.
- Escalate when route, formulation, age, pregnancy status, allergy history, current medicines, or relevant organ-function context is missing.

## Patient Questions to Ask

- What pain, fever, cold, flu, or anti-inflammatory products are you already using?
- Do you have allergies or previous reactions to diclofenac, aspirin, or similar medicines?
- Are you pregnant, trying to become pregnant, or breastfeeding?
- Is the product topical or oral, and are the pharmacist-approved directions clear?

## Knowledge Base Limitations

- This local profile is a limited educational reference for retrieval testing.
- It does not compare diclofenac with all pain or inflammation support options.
- It does not provide patient-specific dosing, clinical assessment, or final treatment decisions.
