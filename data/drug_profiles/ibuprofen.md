# Ibuprofen

This local knowledge-base profile is neutral educational content for pharmacist-support workflows. It is not patient-specific advice and must not be used as a final medication reference.

## Overview

Ibuprofen is a medication commonly discussed in pharmacy settings for pain, inflammation, or fever support. The pharmacist must verify the exact product, strength, route, directions, patient context, and local reference guidance before counseling.

This profile is intended to support retrieval tests and draft counseling workflows. It does not define patient-specific appropriateness or final dosing.

## Common Uses

- Pain or inflammation support may be discussed after pharmacist confirmation of indication and patient-specific risk factors.
- Fever support may be discussed when the pharmacist has confirmed that the product and directions are appropriate for the reviewed case.
- The pharmacist should distinguish ibuprofen from other medicines in the same broad class and review current medication use.

## General Counseling Points

- Ask the patient to follow the final pharmacist-approved label directions.
- Encourage the patient to ask before combining ibuprofen with other pain, fever, cold, flu, or anti-inflammatory products.
- The pharmacist should clarify whether food, timing, duration, or formulation-specific instructions apply to the confirmed product.
- Explain that this draft must be checked against the confirmed prescription and approved references before patient-facing use.

## Safety Notes

- Pharmacist review should include allergy history, pregnancy status, age, stomach or bleeding risk factors, kidney-related context, and current medications.
- Low confidence prescription text, unclear duration, or missing patient context should trigger pharmacist review before counseling.
- This profile does not provide patient-specific dosing, treatment selection, or final advice.

## When to Refer to Pharmacist or Physician

- Refer for pharmacist assessment when the patient reports allergy concerns, pregnancy, stomach bleeding concerns, kidney-related history, or interacting medicines.
- Refer to an appropriate clinician according to local policy when symptoms are severe, persistent, unusual, or outside the pharmacist's scope.
- Escalate when patient age, pregnancy status, allergy history, current medicines, or relevant organ-function context is missing.

## Patient Questions to Ask

- Do you have allergies or previous reactions to ibuprofen, aspirin, or similar medicines?
- Are you pregnant, trying to become pregnant, or breastfeeding?
- Do you have stomach bleeding concerns, kidney-related history, or other medical factors the pharmacist should review?
- What prescription and non-prescription medicines are you currently taking?
