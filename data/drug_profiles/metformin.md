# Metformin

This local knowledge-base profile is neutral educational content for pharmacist-support workflows. It is not patient-specific advice and must not be used as a final medication reference.

## Overview

Metformin is a medication commonly discussed in pharmacy settings for blood glucose support workflows. The pharmacist must verify the exact product, formulation, strength, directions, patient context, and local reference guidance before counseling.

This profile supports local retrieval tests and draft counseling workflows. It does not define patient-specific appropriateness or final use instructions.

## Common Uses

- Blood glucose support counseling may be discussed after the pharmacist confirms the product and patient-specific context.
- The pharmacist should review formulation differences, current medicines, and relevant medical history before patient-facing counseling.
- Condition-only terms should not be treated as a reason to select metformin without pharmacist review.

## General Counseling Points

- Ask the patient to follow the final pharmacist-approved label directions.
- Encourage the patient to ask the pharmacist about formulation-specific instructions for the confirmed product.
- Remind the patient to discuss stomach-related concerns, missed-use questions, or unclear directions with the pharmacist.
- Explain that this draft must be checked against the confirmed product and approved references before patient-facing use.

## Safety Notes

- Pharmacist review should include kidney-related context, current medications, allergy history, age, pregnancy status when relevant, and stomach-related tolerance concerns.
- Low confidence product text, unclear formulation, or missing patient context should trigger pharmacist review before counseling.
- This profile does not confirm patient suitability or final treatment decisions.

## When to Refer to Pharmacist or Physician

- Refer for pharmacist assessment when the patient reports unclear directions, stomach-related concerns, multiple medicines, or kidney-related history.
- Refer to an appropriate clinician according to local policy when symptoms are severe, unusual, persistent, or outside the pharmacist's scope.
- Escalate when kidney-related context, current medicines, age, pregnancy status, or relevant medical context is missing.

## Patient Questions to Ask

- What blood glucose medicines, supplements, or non-prescription products are you currently using?
- Do you have kidney-related history or stomach-related concerns the pharmacist should review?
- Are you pregnant, planning pregnancy, or breastfeeding?
- Are the product name, formulation, and pharmacist-approved directions clear?

## Knowledge Base Limitations

- This local profile is a limited educational reference for retrieval testing.
- It does not assess blood glucose control, therapy selection, or monitoring plans.
- It does not provide patient-specific dosing, clinical assessment, or final treatment decisions.
