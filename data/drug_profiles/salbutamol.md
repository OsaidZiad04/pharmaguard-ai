# Salbutamol

This local knowledge-base profile is neutral educational content for pharmacist-support workflows. It is not patient-specific advice and must not be used as a final medication reference.

## Overview

Salbutamol is a medication commonly discussed in pharmacy settings for reliever inhaler counseling workflows. The pharmacist must verify the exact product, device type, strength, route, directions, patient context, inhaler technique, and local reference guidance before counseling.

This profile supports local retrieval tests and draft counseling workflows. It does not define patient-specific appropriateness, emergency care decisions, or final use instructions.

## Common Uses

- Reliever inhaler counseling may be discussed after the pharmacist confirms the product, device, and patient-specific context.
- The pharmacist should review inhaler technique, current respiratory medicines, symptom pattern, and whether urgent assessment is needed.
- Condition-only terms should not be treated as a reason to select salbutamol without pharmacist review.

## General Counseling Points

- Ask the patient to follow the final pharmacist-approved label directions.
- Encourage the patient to demonstrate or review inhaler technique with the pharmacist when appropriate.
- Remind the patient to ask the pharmacist about device-specific instructions, cleaning, priming, or spacer questions for the confirmed product.
- Explain that this draft must be checked against the confirmed product and approved references before patient-facing use.

## Safety Notes

- Pharmacist review should include age, symptom pattern, current respiratory medicines, allergies, pregnancy status when relevant, and device technique.
- Frequent symptoms, worsening symptoms, or unclear reliever use should trigger pharmacist review and possible escalation according to local policy.
- This profile does not confirm control status, emergency instructions, or final treatment decisions.

## When to Refer to Pharmacist or Physician

- Refer for pharmacist assessment when symptoms are worsening, frequent, unusual, or the patient is uncertain about inhaler technique.
- Refer to an appropriate clinician or urgent service according to local policy when symptoms suggest urgent assessment may be needed.
- Escalate when current medicines, symptom pattern, device type, age, pregnancy status, or relevant medical context is missing.

## Patient Questions to Ask

- What respiratory medicines and inhaler devices are you currently using?
- How often are symptoms occurring, and has the pattern changed recently?
- Can you show how you use the inhaler so the pharmacist can review technique?
- Are the product name, device instructions, and pharmacist-approved directions clear?

## Knowledge Base Limitations

- This local profile is a limited educational reference for retrieval testing.
- It does not assess respiratory condition severity or control status.
- It does not provide patient-specific dosing, clinical assessment, or final treatment decisions.
